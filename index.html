An honest account of my experience with Discover Bank

Published: December 2025 Company: Discover Bank

When Scam Happens, Discover Bank Doesn't Have Your Back

I lost $700 to scammers. Discover Bank's response? "It's not disputable. You're on your own."

Let me be clear: I fell victim to a sophisticated job scam. The scammers directed me to use Cash App to send money as part of what I believed was a legitimate employment requirement. I was trying to keep a job I thought was real. Instead, I was being scammed.

As soon as I realized what happened, I immediately contacted Discover Bank. I thought this was exactly what credit card protection was for—to help customers when they're victims of fraud. I was wrong.

Discover Bank's response was immediate and final: "Because you used Cash App, this transaction is not disputable. We can't help you."

That's it. No investigation. No attempt to understand the situation. No consideration that this was a scam, not a voluntary transaction. Just a blanket policy that protects scammers, not customers.

I spent hours on the phone with multiple representatives and two different supervisors—Ben Wilkinson and Kay. The conversations were infuriating. They kept repeating the same script: "Cash App transactions are peer-to-peer transfers and not disputable." When I explained this wasn't a peer-to-peer transaction—I was sending money to what I believed was an employer as part of a job requirement—they didn't care. The policy was the policy.

Here's what really shows how unreliable Discover Bank is: They couldn't even send me their own policy documents via email. Ben promised to email me the official rules, then switched to mailing it (which takes 2 weeks). When I called back, Kay gave me completely different information and refused to provide her last name or employee number. Two supervisors, two different stories, zero accountability.

They told me my only options were to contact the Better Business Bureau or my state's attorney general. Think about that: Discover Bank, a major financial institution, told me to go complain to government agencies instead of helping me themselves. They literally gave up on their own customer.

The most frustrating part? They acknowledged I was a victim of a scam, but then said it didn't matter because I "participated in the transaction." So according to Discover Bank, if scammers trick you into sending money, that's your fault, not theirs. They have no responsibility to protect you.

I'm sharing this because people need to know: Discover Bank does not protect scam victims. They hide behind policies that favor scammers. They give contradictory information. They refuse to investigate. They blame you for being scammed. And when you ask for help, they tell you to go somewhere else.

If you're considering Discover Bank, ask yourself: Do you want a bank that protects you when something goes wrong, or one that abandons you the moment you need help? My $700 loss taught me that Discover Bank will always choose the latter.
